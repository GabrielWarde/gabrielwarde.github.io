Gabriel WARDE et al.

At URGO RID, we studied harmful molecules like microplastics that can leach from everyday containers into food, drinks, or medicines. Estimating the Execution Time of CUDA Programs

Gabriel WARDE et al.

This report presents machine learning models to predict average and worst-case CUDA kernel execution times for real-time systems. Tested on kernels from the Rodinia benchmark, the models include Linear Regression, Random Forests, Neural Networks, SVMs, and Curve Fitting. Calibration strategies ensure safe WCET estimates with minimal profiling, and the Curve Fit model achieved the best balance of accuracy and conservative overestimation.

Anomaly Detection in Immunofluorescence Microscopy Slides

Gabriel WARDE et al.

Microscopy slides in laboratories frequently yield images with artifacts or blurriness, severely compromising the reliability of subsequent medical AI models. To address this at the CHU de Lille, we developed a robust quality control system by testing many CNN architectures to automatically classify low-quality Anti-Nuclear Antibody (ANA) images. The ultimate goal was to select and deploy the best-performing model to ensure only reliable, high-quality images are used for accurate diagnosis and machine learning models.
